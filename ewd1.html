English Vocabulary
English

更新日期:   2020-06-13

nasal

  • adj. 鼻的；带有鼻音的

mite

  • 螨虫
  • a mite ~
  • poor little mite

inferior

  • No body is inferior to you here.
  • No inferior products should be allowed to pass.

rejoice

  • (~ at/in/over) The motor industry is rejoicing at the cut in car tax.
  • When the war ended, people finally had cause to rejoice.

preliminary

  • (n.) I will skip the usual preliminaries, and come straight to the point.
  • (adj.) After a few preliminary remarks he announced the winners.

conscious

  • (adj.) be ~ of (doing) sth. She is acutely conscious of the severeness of the problem.

pride and prejudice

bride and groom

predecessor and successor

relate

  • relate a tail story.(narrate)

relative

  • n. 亲戚
  • adj. 相对的，相关的 (~ to)

relativity

  • n. 相关性

relevant

  • (irrelevant) 紧密相关的，切题的
  • These comments are not directly relevant to this inquiry.
  • 有价值的，有意义的（relevance）
  • I don’t see the relevance of your question.

overlook

  • She has been overlooked for promotion several times.
  • We could not afford to overlook such a serious offense.
  • a restaurant overlooking the lake.

overseas /~si:z/

  • adj. 海外的 ~ development/markets
  • adv. to work overseas.

apt

  • an apt description/comment
  • be apt to do sth. Babies are apt to put objects into their mouths.

mock

  • v. 嘲笑
  • adj. 虚假的
  • adj. 模拟的，模仿的

premier

  • n. 总理
  • adj. 最好的，最重要的

displace

  • Gradually, factory workers have been displaced by machines.
  • Around 10,000 people have been displaced by the continuous fighting.

He is a genius and invented a lot of ingenious devices.

prestige

  • adj. a prestige job
  • n. There is a lot of prestige attached to owning a car like this.

presumably

  • 可能；大概
  • Presumably this is where the accident happened.

Now predator and prey live in harmony.

We need to cut back our reliance on fossil fuels.

cut back: spend less, use less or do less

strenuous

  • Avoid strenuous exercises immediately after a meal.
  • The government strenuously denies the allegation.

allegation

  • Several newspapers made allegations of corruption in the city’s police department
  • to invest/deny/withdraw an allegation

transaction

  • financial/commercial transactions
  • IEEE transactions（学报，会报）

transient

  • 短暂的

arise

  • 出现，发生
  • Children should be ==disciplined== when the need arises.( when it is necessary.)
  • arise from 有…造成
  • 起床，起立

arouse

  • to arouse sb.’s interest/curiosity/anger/suspicion
  • arousal (sexual arousal)

The agreement was declared void.

The sky is void of stars.

dispute

  • 质疑
  • 争辩；争端；disputed territory

dissolve

  • 溶解
  • 解散，散开，解除（关系）
  • Their marriage was dissolved in 1999.

exchange

  • 交换
  • 交谈，争论（conversation/argument）
  • 兑换

exclusive

  • 独家的，专用的
  • 排他的
  • 高档的

excessive，immoderate 过度的， moderate
